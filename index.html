QuizPliz Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 17, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the QuizPliz mobile application (the “App”) for iOS and Android handles information. The App is provided by Alisher Serikbayev (“we,” “us,” or “our”). By using the App you agree to the practices described here.

QuizPliz is an offline-first trivia game. It does not require an account, a login, or any sign-up, and we do not ask you for your name, email address, or other directly identifying information.

1. Information stored on your device

Your game data — scores, best score, progress and tier, daily-streak history, and your language choice — is stored locally on your device. We do not collect it, it is not transmitted to us, and it is deleted when you uninstall the App.

2. Information collected automatically by third-party services

The App uses the following third-party services from Google, which may collect information automatically to provide their functionality:

  • Google AdMob — serves the ads shown in the App. AdMob may collect and use device advertising identifiers and ad-interaction data to deliver and measure ads. See How Google uses advertising data.
  • Firebase Analytics (Google) — collects analytics about how the App is used. This includes a limited set of events we log (for example: opening the App, starting a game, clearing a level, an ad revive, ending a game, completing the daily challenge, and sharing a score), together with data Firebase collects automatically by default — such as app sessions, device model, operating-system version, and an approximate region derived from your IP address. We use this to understand overall engagement. We do not log the individual questions you answer.
  • Firebase Crashlytics (Google) — if the App crashes, it collects a crash report with diagnostic and device-state information so we can fix the problem.

Information collected by these services is handled under Google’s Privacy Policy. We do not control, and are not responsible for, Google’s independent data practices.

3. How information is used

  • To operate, maintain, and improve the App.
  • To display and measure advertisements.
  • To diagnose crashes and improve stability and performance.

We do not sell your personal information.

4. Advertising choices

Ads in the App are provided by Google AdMob. You can review and limit ad personalization and tracking through your device settings at any time:

  • iOS: the App asks for permission to track using Apple’s App Tracking Transparency. You can decline (“Ask App Not to Track”) or change your choice at any time under Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking. When app tracking is not allowed, the ads you see are non-personalized.
  • Android: reset or delete your advertising ID and opt out of personalized ads under Settings → Google → Ads.

5. Children’s privacy

QuizPliz is intended for a general audience and is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided information through the App, contact us and we will take appropriate steps.

6. U.S. state privacy rights (including California)

We do not sell your personal information. Depending on your state of residence (for example, California under the CCPA/CPRA), you may have the right to know what personal information is collected, to request deletion, and to opt out of the “sale” or “sharing” of personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. Because we do not require an account, the most direct ways to exercise these choices are the device-level ad controls in Section 4 and uninstalling the App to remove locally stored data. You may also contact us using the details below.

7. Data retention

On-device game data persists until you delete the App. Data collected by Google (analytics, crash reports, advertising data) is retained according to Google’s own retention policies.

8. Availability

The App is offered in the United States and other regions, and is not offered to users in the European Economic Area (EEA) or the United Kingdom.

9. Security

We take reasonable measures to protect information. Most of your data stays on your device, and information handled by Google’s services is protected under their security practices. No method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes take effect when posted on this page, and the “Last updated” date above will be revised.
